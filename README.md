# AI-Driven Large Language Models in Further Education: Impact on Teaching Practices (UK) 

## MSc. Data Science Project in colaboration with Teaching and Learning Lab of New City College 



This project analyses how Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, are influencing teaching practices in UK Further Education institutions.

Using survey data from educators, the study explores adoption behaviour, perceived benefits, challenges, and future intentions regarding AI-assisted teaching.

The aim is to translate educator responses into **data-driven insights for education policy and institutional decision-making**.

---

## Key Insights (Read First)

- Most educators report a positive impact of AI on lesson planning efficiency  
- AI adoption is strongly influenced by digital literacy levels  
- Main barriers include academic integrity concerns and over-reliance risks  
- More experienced teachers tend to be slightly more cautious with AI adoption  
- Overall intention to adopt AI in future is high across respondents  

---

## Business / Institutional Impact

This project provides actionable insights for:

- UK Further Education institutions  
- Education policy makers  
- Teaching and learning development teams  

### Recommendations:
- Invest in structured AI literacy training for educators  
- Develop clear academic integrity policies for AI use  
- Encourage guided integration of AI tools into teaching practice rather than informal use

## Dataset Overview

- Source: Online survey of UK Further Education educators  
- Type: Structured questionnaire data  
- Size: *700+ FE educators from NCC *  
- Ethics: All responses anonymised to ensure GDPR compliance  

### Variables:
- Teaching experience (years)  
- Frequency of AI usage  
- Perceived usefulness of LLMs  
- Concerns regarding AI implementation  
- Future adoption intentions  

---

## Methodology

### 1. Data Cleaning
- Handled missing values  
- Standardised categorical responses  
- Converted survey responses into analysable formats  

### 2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Distribution of AI usage across educators  
- Relationship between teaching experience and AI adoption  
- Comparison of perceptions vs usage patterns  

### 3. Statistical Analysis
- Correlation analysis between variables  
- Hypothesis testing on adoption behaviour differences  

### 4. Data Visualisation
- Bar charts for usage frequency  
- Heatmaps for correlation analysis  
- Comparative plots for key variables
